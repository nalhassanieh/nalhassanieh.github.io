My research lies in the fields of numerical analysis, complex analysis, PDEs, and scientific computing. 

During my PhD studies, I worked on finite difference methods for PDEs on complex geometries. I used overset grids to discretize complicated geometry and introduced novel boundary closures to support high-order accurate finite difference schemes. 

For my postdoctoral work, I investigated integral equation methods and spectral methods. I developed algorithms for the rapid and high-order accurate evaluation of wave-equation integral solution representations in one, two, and three dimensions, in free space, and with periodic boundary conditions. These algorithms aid in the development of wave-scattering schemes. My research work relies heavily on analysis skills, where I study accuracy, stability, and complexity of algorithms, and computational skills, where I develop, test, and scale these algorithms.

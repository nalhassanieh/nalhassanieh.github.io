I’m interested in designing fast algorithms to simulate waves scattering off objects in time and space. These algorithms accelerate solutions to problems in fields such as acoustics, structural health monitoring, seismology, and medical imaging. They can be used to design products, detect defects, predict outcomes, and understand physical phenomena. In particular, my research lies in the fields of numerical analysis, complex analysis, PDEs, and scientific computing. During my PhD studies, I worked on finite difference methods for PDEs on complex geometries. I used overset grids to discretize complicated geometry and introduced novel boundary closures to support high-order accurate interior schemes. For my postdoctoral work, I investigated integral equation methods and spectral methods. I developed algorithms for the rapid and high-order accurate evaluation of time-domain wave-equation integral solution representations in one, two, and three dimensions. These algorithms aid in the development of fast wave-scattering schemes. My research work relies heavily on analysis skills, where I study accuracy, stability, and complexity of algorithms, and computational skills, where I develop, test, and scale these algorithms.

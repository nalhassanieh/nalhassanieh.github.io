My research lies in the fields of numerical analysis, complex analysis, PDEs, and scientific computing. 

During my PhD studies, I worked on finite difference methods for PDEs on complex geometries. I used overset grids to discretize complex geometry and introduced novel boundary closures to support high-order-accurate finite-difference schemes. 

I currently work on developing fast, accurate algorithms for solving wave-equation problems using time-domain boundary integral methods. My goal is to design fast and accurate schemes for 2D and 3D wave-equation scattering problems arising in applications such as acoustics, electromagnetics, and geophysics.
